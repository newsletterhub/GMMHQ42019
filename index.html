Is the NYC residential market showing signs of stabilization?
I'm pleased to announce the release of the Compass 4Q2019 Manhattan Market Report for your review.
 
In 4Q2019, we continue to see a buyer's market as evidenced by the record average days on market (168) as well as average price discounts (10%) versus 4Q2018. However, there are signs of positive market momentum as closed sales increased slightly (+1%), reversing past quarterly declines, active listing inventory is lower (-2.4%), and unit absorption is greater (overall months of supply has declined slightly). 
 
In the 4Q, apartments priced $3M and under comprised 88% of all unit sales. This market segment remains active and buyers seem more optimistic, reflecting, in part, record low mortgage rates as well as a healthy NYC economy with a growing tech sector.
In a market with abundant supply (including new development luxury condos) and a climate of uncertainty, buyers are exercising caution but also recognizing purchase opportunities. As more sellers price strategically or are willing to discount to meet buyers demands on price, expectations are for further increased contract activity leading to future market stabilization.

Note: The Compass report addresses resale activity only. There are currently an estimated 7,000+ unsold new development condo units (including buildings still under construction as well as shadow inventory) in Manhattan, according to Halstead Development Marketing. With absorption at its current rate, that represents 74 months of inventory or more than six years.

Highlights of the fourth-quarter report are as follows:
 
CLOSED SALES
 
  • There were 2,676 recorded or closed sales in 4Q2019, a 1% increase from the same period in the prior year, reversing a trend of quarterly declines.
     
  • The median sale price decreased 3% to $999,000 from $1,030,000 in 2018, while the average sale price decreased 9.5% to $1,862,523 from $2,058,373 last year.
     
  • Sales priced between $1-$3M represented the greatest share of closings in the 4Q2019, comprising 38% of all deals. The $500K-$1M segment followed closely with 37%. Average price discounts held at about 9% for properties priced from $500K-$3M.
  • 1-BR units comprised 40% of all transactions with an average $1,183 PPSF and an average size of 813 square feet. 2-BR units were the second highest with 30% of the market and an average of $1,374 PPSF and an average size of 1,374 square feet.
    Studios accounted for 12% of the market and saw the lowest average PPSF of $1,157.
     
  • Downtown captured 30% of the market with an average PPSF of $1,677 and average size of 1,444 square feet. The Upper West Side and the Upper East Side followed with 19% and 18% market share, respectively. The UWS had an average PPSF of $1,240 and average size of 1,185 square feet while the UES had an average PPSF of $1,225 and average size of 1,423 square feet.
 
SIGNED CONTRACTS
 
  • The number of signed contracts increased 4% in the 4Q2019. Properties spent an average 168 days on the market, the longest average since 2012. Downtown accounted for a majority of signed contract activity at 28% and the Upper West Side and Upper East Side followed at 21% and 20%, respectively. 
     
  • 1-BR units comprised 37% of all contracts signed in the 4Q. 2-BR units made up 31% of the total, while 3-BR trailed further behind at 14%. 1-BR units were 11% more affordable on average at $1,247/sf vs. 4Q2018 and 2-BR units were 7% more affordable at $1,447/sf.
     
  • With 28% of all contracts signed in the first 59 days, properties under $500K (13%) moved the fastest. Properties in the $500K-$1M (34%) saw 32% go into contract during the same period, with properties in the $1-$3M (38%) bracket trailing behind at 24%. The slowest moving units were in the luxury price segment $3-$10M (13%) with approximately 50% on the market for at least 180 days+.
     
  • Upper Manhattan (9%) was the top performing neighborhood with 32% of signed contract units in less than 59 days and Midtown West (3%), exhibiting the worst performance at 16%.
 
ACTIVE LISTING INVENTORY
 
  • 72% of active inventory was priced below $3M, and 36% between $1-$3M. The $10-20M and $20M+ brackets captured only 4% and 2%, respectively, their lowest since 2011.
     
  • The $1-3M segment offered larger, less expensive properties on average, growing 2% in size and decreasing 2% in average PPSF (for condos; for co-ops, size increased on average 4% and price declined by 7%). This trend was also observed to some extent in units priced $3-5M.
     
  • Downtown continued to hold the largest share of the market with 28% of all current listings, and had the largest and most expensive units on average (2,019 average square feet and $2,063 average PPSF), though both metrics declined slightly compared to 4Q2018. The Upper East Side followed with 19% of inventory and an average size of 1,930 sf (-7% YOY) and average PPSF of $1,689 (average price PPSF of condos at $1,987 and co-ops at $1,234).
     
  • In regard to co-ops, the Upper East Side, Upper West Side, and Midtown East, were more affordable compared to this time last year, seeing a 6%, 2%, and 6% decline in average PPSF, respectively.
 
  • Overall, active listings declined by 2.4% to 6,993 units (vs. 7,162 units in 4Q 2018) and months of supply decreased to 7.4 (vs. 7.8 in the 4Q2018). Indicators demonstrate the market is stabilizing and sellers have become more realistic in regard to pricing their homes, and in many cases, responding to buyer demands with price discounts in order to close sales.
 
(Note: Months of supply represents the number of months it would take to absorb all active inventory based on the trailing 12 months average of contract activity. Typically 6 to 9 months of supply indicates market equilibrium. Please note this analysis does not include ‘shadow inventory’ of unsold, unlisted new development inventory.)
